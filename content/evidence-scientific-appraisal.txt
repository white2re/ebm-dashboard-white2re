## Systematic Biases and Limitations
Evidence shows moderate bias risks including geographic limitations (Ghana and Indonesia only), industry restrictions (banking and retail sectors), and potential publication bias favoring positive compensation-satisfaction relationships. Temporal bias is low with contemporary 2020s research.

## Evidence Strength Assessment
Limited quantity (2 studies, N=180) but medium quality with high consistency across industries. Cross-sectional designs prevent causal inference and effect size magnitude unknown due to qualitative reporting. High relevance for compensation interventions targeting motivation and job satisfaction outcomes.

## Confidence in Evidence

### For Problem Definition
- **Evidence Strength:** Moderate
- **Confidence Level:** Medium confidence
- **Key Limitations:** Cross-sectional designs cannot establish that compensation deficits cause motivation problems; limited geographic and industry representation; lack of longitudinal validation

### For Solution Effectiveness
- **Evidence Strength:** Moderate
- **Confidence Level:** Medium confidence  
- **Key Limitations:** No numeric effect sizes limit intervention planning precision; single-organization studies reduce generalizability; cross-sectional design cannot confirm sustained intervention effects

## Research Gaps and Future Needs
Critical gaps include lack of longitudinal studies, missing intervention dosage guidance, and absence of numeric effect sizes. Future research needs longitudinal designs, broader industry representation, and randomized trials with quantitative reporting for precise intervention planning.

## Implications for Decision Making

Moderate weight (40-50% of decision framework) - Scientific evidence provides valuable directional validation of compensation ‚Üí motivation ‚Üí job satisfaction pathway but methodological limitations require complementing with organizational and practitioner evidence

**Requires Caution:**
