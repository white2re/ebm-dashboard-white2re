# Practitioner Evidence: Methods & Acquisition Strategy
**Logic Model:** Compensation → Motivation → Job Satisfaction

## How Practitioner Perspectives Can Be Gathered

Practitioner insights would be collected through interviews with compensation and HR professionals, supplemented by surveys for more validation.

## Sources to Consult

### Primary Sources
- **Industry Associations:** Society for Human Resource Management (SHRM), World at Work compensation professionals, International Association of Business Communicators (IABC)
- **Professional Networks:** LinkedIn compensation and benefits groups, HR executive alumni networks, regional SHRM chapters
- **Direct Outreach:** Targeted outreach to Chief People Officers, Compensation Directors, and Total Rewards Managers with demonstrated experience in motivation-focused compensation design
- **Conference Connections:** WorldatWork Total Rewards Conference attendees, SHRM Annual Conference practitioners, regional HR symposium speakers

### Secondary Sources
- **Industry Publications:** Harvard Business Review compensation articles, SHRM Total Rewards research reports, Compensation & Benefits Review journal
- **Case Studies:** Published organizational examples of successful compensation redesign from McKinsey, Deloitte, and PwC consulting reports
- **White Papers:** World at Work research on pay-for-performance effectiveness, Willis Towers Watson employee engagement survey insights
- **Professional Blogs:** Compensation thought leadership from Josh Bersin, Compensation Cafe, and HR Executive compensation experts

## Selection Criteria

### Practitioner Qualifications
- **Experience Level:** Minimum 7-15 years in compensation design, total rewards, or organizational development roles
- **Role Requirements:** Director-level or above in Compensation, Total Rewards, People Operations, or Chief People Officer positions with budget authority for compensation decisions
- **Industry Focus:** Professional services, technology, financial services, and retail sectors to match scientific evidence contexts
- **Organization Size:** Mid-size to large organizations (500-10,000+ employees) with structured compensation programs and measurable employee engagement data
- **Geographic Scope:** North America and Europe to complement scientific evidence from Ghana and Indonesia, providing global perspective

### Relevance Criteria
- **Problem Experience:** Direct experience addressing low employee motivation and job satisfaction through compensation interventions
- **Solution Implementation:** Documented experience implementing compensation changes specifically designed to enhance motivation and retention
- **Measurable Results:** Ability to provide before/after metrics on employee engagement, satisfaction scores, or turnover reduction following compensation improvements
- **Transferability:** Experience in organizational contexts with professional employee populations similar to target intervention environment

## Recruitment Strategy

Practitioners would be identified through professional networks (SHRM, World at Work), industry conferences, and LinkedIn outreach targeting experienced compensation and total rewards professionals.

## AI Assistance in Evidence Gathering

AI would assist with identifying relevant compensation practitioners and publications, with all findings to be validated through human review and professional verification.
